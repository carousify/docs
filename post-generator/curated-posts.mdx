Curated Posts is a feed of LinkedIn post ideas Carousify writes for you. It learns from your connected LinkedIn account and posts fresh ideas to the **Curated Posts / Inspiration** tab in the sidebar on the days you pick.

## Turn it on

Curated Posts is configured per workspace under **Manage Workspace › General › AI Assistant**.

<img
  src="/images/workspaces/ai-assistant.png"
  alt="AI Assistant section in workspace settings showing the AI Content Assistant toggle, account, and days fields"
  className="rounded-xl border"
/>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Toggle AI Content Assistant on">
    The account and day pickers appear once it's on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the LinkedIn account">
    Pick which connected account Carousify should mine for niche signals. Your activity and audience drive what shows up in the feed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the days">
    Select which days Carousify should generate fresh curated posts. New ideas land on those days — pick the ones you actually plan to write.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Click **Save Changes**. The first batch takes a few hours to generate; after that the feed refreshes on the days you picked.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Open the feed

Once enabled, click **Curated Posts / Inspiration** in the left sidebar.

<img
  src="/images/post-generator/curated-posts.png"
  alt="Curated Posts page showing the empty state before generation completes"
  className="rounded-xl border"
/>

If the page is empty, either generation hasn't finished yet (give it a few hours after first turning the toggle on) or the AI Content Assistant is off. Use **Change AI Preference** at the top to jump back to settings.

## Use the ideas

Each curated post is an idea draft you can:

- **Open** in the editor and refine before publishing
- **Schedule** straight to LinkedIn from the post like any other Carousify post
- **Skip** if it's not the angle you want — Carousify keeps refreshing on your chosen days

<Tip>
  Treat the feed like a writing prompt list, not a publish-as-is queue. The faster you edit and ship, the better the next batch gets at matching your voice.
</Tip>
